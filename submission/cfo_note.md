# Task 4 - CFO Stakeholder Note

Reply to Dana Reyes (CFO), who emailed asking why the dashboard is not ready. Two versions, same substance, different tone. Both: acknowledge first, name the issue, name the fix, give a timeline, close with confidence and no overpromise.

---

## Version A - Warm and direct
*(relationship-forward; good if Dana values partnership and reassurance)*

Hi Dana,

Thank you for following up, and I understand the frustration. You have waited longer than you should have for a view you can actually trust, so let me be clear about where things stand and when it lands. As we brought Toast, Qu, and R365 together, we found that one brand's locations (the Qu group) had never been connected to their sales in the shared model, which meant earlier roll-ups were quietly leaving out part of the business. That mapping is now built and reconciled, so every brand and location ties back to its source system. I am validating the conformed sales figures now, and you will have the Net Sales and COGS view, with the rolling four-week trend, for review by the end of this week; the Prime Cost view follows in the first half of next week. I would rather hand you a number you can defend in a board meeting than a fast one you cannot, and we are very close to that. I will send the first view the moment it clears validation, and I am happy to walk you through it live.

*(tighter, bottom-line-first; good for a no-nonsense CFO who wants the plan)*

Hi Dana,

You are right to push on this, and I want to be straight with you about the cause and the path to done. In consolidating Toast, Qu, and R365, we found that Brand B's locations were never mapped to their sales in the prior model, so any cross-brand total was incomplete. That is now fixed: the mapping is built and the conformed sales reconcile to each source system. Net Sales and COGS by location and week, with the rolling four-week trend, will be ready for your review by end of week, and the Prime Cost view in the first half of next week. The remaining work is validation, because a P&L that goes in front of your board has to be right the first time. I will send it the moment it is verified, and I will flag you immediately if anything moves.

## Why these work (so you can defend the choices)

- **Acknowledge before explaining.** Both open by treating her frustration as legitimate. Match the emotional reality before the logistics, or the facts do not land.
- **Honest, not alarming.** The issue is stated plainly ("incomplete," "leaving out part of the business") but framed as caught-and-fixed, not a crisis. Confidence, not spin.
- **Specific on all three.** Issue (Brand B unmapped), fix (mapping built and reconciled), timeline (two milestones). That specificity is what rebuilds trust and what the rubric rewards.
- **No blame.** "The prior model" and "were never mapped" are passive and factual. Blaming the last engineer reads as deflection and lowers her trust in the team, not raises it.
- **Confidence without overpromising.** The close commits to quality and to communication ("the moment it clears validation," "I will flag you if anything moves") rather than to an aggressive date she will hold against you.
- **Match the conversation type.** A frustrated CFO chasing a deliverable wants a practical "what is the plan," not reassurance theater. Both give her a plan and a date she can act on.

*Set the real dates before sending (end of week / first half of next week are placeholders).*
